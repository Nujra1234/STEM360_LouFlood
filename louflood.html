Louisville Flood Survival Guide

Based on FEMA guidelines, Louisville Metro Emergency Services protocols, and lessons from the April 2025 Ohio River flood — the 8th worst in Louisville history. This guide covers what to do before, during, and after a flood event.

📅
Before a Flood
1

Know your flood zone

Check if your address is in a FEMA Zone A floodplain at apps.lojic.org or Louisville MSD's floodplain tool.

2

Sign up for CodeRED alerts

Register your phone at louisvilleky.gov to receive automatic emergency notifications for Jefferson County.

3

Plan two evacuation routes

River Rd and low-lying roads near Beargrass Creek close first. Know alternate routes away from the Ohio River.

4

Build a go-bag

Pack it now — don't wait until the flood watch is issued.

3 days of water (1 gal/person/day)
Non-perishable food + can opener
Prescription medications (7-day supply)
Phone charger + battery bank
Important documents (waterproofed)
First aid kit
Flashlight + extra batteries
Cash (ATMs fail during power outages)
Change of clothes + warm layers
Whistle (signal for help if trapped)
🚨
During a Flood
1

Evacuate early — don't wait

When LouFlood Alert shows 🟠 Warning or higher, leave flood-zone homes immediately. The river rises fast.

2

Move to higher ground

Head to the Highlands, St. Matthews, or east Louisville which sit above typical flood levels. Avoid roads along the Ohio River or Beargrass Creek.

3

If trapped indoors — go up

Move to the highest floor. Do NOT go into a closed attic — bring a tool to break through the roof if needed. Signal for help from a window.

4

If swept into water

Don't fight the current. Float on your back with feet downstream. Grab anything fixed (tree, post). Call 911 as soon as possible.

⚠ NEVER DO THESE THINGS
  • Drive through flooded roadways — 12" of water sweeps away a car
  • Walk through moving floodwater — 6" can knock you down
  • Touch electrical equipment in flooded areas
  • Return home until officials declare it safe
  • Use generators or grills indoors (carbon monoxide)
🔄
After a Flood
1

Wait for official clearance

Don't return home until Louisville Metro Emergency Services declares your area safe. Floodwater is toxic.

2

Document all damage

Photograph everything before cleaning up. This is required for FEMA assistance and insurance claims.

3

Apply for FEMA assistance

Jefferson County residents can apply at disasterassistance.gov or visit a Disaster Recovery Center. After April 2025, FEMA approved individual assistance for Jefferson County.

4

Request debris pickup

Submit the Louisville Metro Self-Reporting Form at louisvilleky.gov to request curbside flood debris pickup.

5

Check for mold within 24–48 hours

Floodwater leaves bacteria and promotes mold growth rapidly. Wear gloves and a mask when re-entering. Remove wet materials quickly.

📍
Louisville-Specific Tips
!

Know which roads close first

River Road (entire length near the Ohio), Northwestern Pkwy near Shawnee Park, and low sections of Story Ave near Beargrass Creek flood first and close quickly.

!

Watch Beargrass Creek separately

Beargrass Creek through Butchertown and NuLu can flood independently of the Ohio River during heavy rain, even without a river flood warning.

!

The Louisville Floodwall protects downtown

The MSD floodwall protects the downtown core up to certain levels — but Portland, Shawnee, and areas west of downtown are outside the wall's protection.

!

Monitor KY Mesonet upstream

Heavy rain at CRMT (Bullitt Co.) or OLDA (Oldham Co.) stations typically raises the Ohio River level 6–12 hours later. Watch this dashboard's Mesonet panel for early warning.
